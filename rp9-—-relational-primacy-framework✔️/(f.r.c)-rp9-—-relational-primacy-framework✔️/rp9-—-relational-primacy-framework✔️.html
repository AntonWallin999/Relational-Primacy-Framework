DOCUMENT I — Formal Delimitation


Status

Non-ground protective document.
This document is not part of the foundation and introduces no causal structure.

Purpose

The sole purpose of this document is to formally delimit the scope and role of
Axiom R0 — Relational Necessity within RP9.

It exists to prevent:

  • misclassification,
  • accidental derivation,
  • and unauthorized extension of the foundation.

This document introduces no claims of its own.

Foundational Statement

Axiom R0 is the only non-derivative statement in RP9.

Everything else in the framework is:

  • dependent,
  • descriptive,
  • classificatory,
  • or interpretative.

This document does not add to the axiom.
It only specifies what follows if the axiom is assumed.

What the Axiom Does Not Introduce

Axiom R0 introduces:

  • no objects
  • no functions
  • no operations
  • no measures
  • no representations
  • no domains of application

It establishes only a relational necessity condition.

Non-derivation Rule

No statement within RP9 may be treated as:

  • co-axiomatic,
  • implicitly foundational,
  • or independently necessary

other than Axiom R0 itself.

All other documents are strictly secondary.

Metastatus Clarification

This document:

  • does not assert that Axiom R0 is true
  • does not defend the axiom
  • does not justify the axiom

It only specifies the logical consequences of assuming it.

Equivalent Linguistic Restatements (Non-generative)

The following are restatements, not consequences:

  • An undifferentiated state cannot function.
  • Function presupposes relation.
  • Relation presupposes at least two distinct poles.

These formulations:

  • add no structure,
  • introduce no causality,
  • and are not independently operative.

Collapse Condition

If Axiom R0 is denied:

  • the concepts of relation, differentiation, and function lose definition
  • all subsequent documents lose semantic grounding
  • the framework collapses entirely

No part of RP9 survives independently of the axiom.

Final Lock

RP9 therefore contains:

  • one axiom
  • zero foundational functions
  • zero free parameters
  • zero derivation chains at ground level

All remaining text is:

  • organizational,
  • classificatory,
  • or explanatory only.

The foundation stands alone.
Everything else is order.

Contributing to RP9 — Relational Primacy Framework


Thank you for your interest in RP9.

This repository is not a conventional open-source project.
It is a causally structured reference framework with a locked relational core.

As such, contribution is intentionally restricted.

Scope of contributions

❌ Not accepted

  • Casual pull requests
  • Feature additions
  • Refactoring or stylistic changes
  • Alternative axioms or foundational modifications
  • “Clarifications” that alter meaning or scope

The core framework (Axiom 0, Definitions, Theorems, Relational Sheets A–C) is not open to modification.

✅ Accepted (by request only)

  • Precise objections to internal coherence
  • Identification of hidden assumptions
  • Structural or categorical errors
  • Formal critiques of derivations

A single decisive objection is more valuable than extensive commentary.

How to engage

If you believe you have identified a structural flaw or inconsistency:

  1. Do not submit a pull request.
  2. Open an issue, or contact the author directly.
  3. State the objection clearly and minimally.
  4. Reference the exact document and section.

No defense, debate, or iteration is expected unless explicitly requested.

“Silence or non-response does not constitute acceptance or endorsement.”


Philosophy

RP9 defines a closed relational circuit as cause.
All further systems, interpretations, and applications are open consequences.

This repository preserves the cause.
Contributions are evaluated solely on whether they identify errors in that cause.

Attribution

If you build upon RP9 externally, attribution is required under the project license.

  • LEVEL 2 refers to plural functional potentials: actualized and combined possibilities.

The appendix describes the necessary cycle:

potential → actualization → stabilization → new potential

as a consequence of relational differentiation.

A.1 — Definition: Differentiation

Differentiation denotes relational distinction.

Without differentiation:

  • no relation
  • no potential
  • no function

Differentiation is therefore a necessary condition, not a process.

A.2 — Corollary R0-P

Relational potential and its actualization

Given Axiom R0, it follows:

Every functioning relation contains an inherent potential that is not yet actualized.

This potential consists of:

  • differentiation without process
  • asymmetry without movement
  • tension without manifestation

The potential is relational, not substantial.

Actualization

Actualization occurs when relational potential turns into function.

This implies:

  • tension is reduced through process
  • differentiation assumes the form of movement
  • potential ceases to be potential at the very moment it is manifested

Actualization is not an addition, but a negation of potential as potential.

Phase Relation

Potential and actualization are:

  • not opposites
  • not separate entities
  • not simultaneous within the same relational phase

They are two phases of the same relation, ordered by structure or time.

Consequence

All manifestation is local and temporary.
All potential is structural and prior.

No actualization can exhaust all potential, since total dissolution of differentiation would result in functional collapse.

  • Potential = relational tension prior to function

Meta-Corollary M0 — On the Millennium Problems and Relational Dissolution

Non-foundation. Epistemic classification.

Status:
This meta-corollary is not part of the foundation.

Statement

Given Axiom R0 and the established leveling structure of RP9, it follows:

The Millennium Problems are not unsolved in the strict sense;
they are misplaced.

They are formulated and treated as if they were foundational problems, even though they arise as secondary artifacts within LEVEL 2.

Classification of the problems

  1. All Millennium Problems are formulated in languages ​​and structures that:

  • assume objects, functions or spaces as primary,

  • treat combinations and fusions as the basis,

  • operate entirely within LEVEL 2.

  1. These problems therefore lack direct coherence with the source (Axiom R0), since:

  • their building blocks are already fragmented,

  • their formalisms are secondary stabilizations,

  • their rules are internally closed but externally non-derivable.

They are formally consistent, but axiomatically decoupled from relational necessity.

Special: The Riemann Problem

The Riemann Hypothesis is the only Millennium Problem that is actually directly manifested in Axiom R0.

This is because:

  • the problem expresses a relational coherence issue, not an isolated computational problem,

  • it concerns distribution, symmetry and differentiation rather than construction,

  • it points towards relational necessity, not towards additional structures.

The Riemann Problem is therefore not a problem in RP9, but:

a manifestation of Axiom R0 within a secondary language.

The Central Paradox

The paradox is strict:

  • The problem cannot be solved with the rules that created it.

  • Those rules are LEVEL-2 rules, built on fragmented relational fusions.

  • Axiom R0 does not allow the source to be proven by its own consequences.

Therefore:

The problem is not accepted as trivial by LEVEL 2,
precisely because it reveals LEVEL 2's own limitation.

This is not a technical obstacle but an axiomatic lock.

Resolution, not solution

RP9 does not offer “solutions” in the classical sense, because:

  • classical solutions require that LEVEL 2 is allowed to normalize its own source,

  • this conflicts with relational necessity.

Instead, RP9 offers:

a mapping of relational coherences and fragmentations,
through which the problems cease to be basic problems.

This is a resolution by reclassification, not by construction.

Final locking

**Problems that arise in secondary fusions
cannot be resolved back to the source with the same formalisms.

When this is established, the problem ceases to be a problem
and appears as a consequence of Axiom R0.**

The central point

  • Alternative 1 (free rules, free constructions):

  • can create arbitrary complexity

  • can create problems

  • can create internal coherences

  • but lacks relational legitimacy relative to Axiom R0

  • and cannot recognize or derive this deficiency from within itself

  • Alternative 2 (Axiom R0):

  • eliminates the problems as fundamental problems

  • can explain why they arise in Alternative 1

  • can map the fragmentation

  • can understand the limitation in Alternative 1

  • but cannot be reduced to Alternative 1

This implies a one-sided derivability.

This is exactly the kind of asymmetry that Axiom R0 describes.

Final formulation

Epistemic choice and hierarchical asymmetry

Two principled approaches are available:

Alternative 1:
Free construction of rules, frameworks, functions and relations to information, without requirement for relational derivation.

Alternative 2:
Following from Axiom R0 and its relational necessity.

The outcome is asymmetrical but unambiguous.

Alternative 1 can generate complexity, problems and internal coherences, but lacks relational legitimacy relative to Axiom R0.
This deficiency does not have to be recognized by Alternative 1 and cannot be derived from within its own rules.

Alternative 2 eliminates the problems as fundamental problems.
At the same time, it enables the understanding of why the problems arise in Alternative 1, and why Alternative 1 lacks the ability to solve them.

Alternative 2 can describe Alternative 1.
Alternative 1 cannot describe Alternative 2.

Hence, there follows a built-in hierarchical function between the systems.

Only one of them reflects the relational structure that both manifest.
Only this system can therefore process both without loss.

This is not a position, but a consequence.

Introductory Presentation — AI-Based Quality Control

Purpose and Method

This quality control is carried out using an advanced language model-based AI system (Google LLM Notebook), not with the aim of replacing human review, but to test in isolation the structural function of question formulations and the formal consistency of the answers.

The central object of the review is not the AI ​​itself, but the questions.
The AI ​​system is used here as a methodological instrument to determine whether the questions, as formulated, necessarily lead to traceable, consistent and regular answers – or whether they allow for interpretive slippage, bias or semantic evasions.

Why AI is used in this quality function

A sufficiently advanced AI system has a specific and limited advantage in this context:

  • It has no self-interest in the outcome

  • It lacks personal investment in theories, traditions or positions

  • It is not bound to human disciplinary areas or established interpretive patterns

This does not mean that AI “knows more” than a human.
This means that AI does not automatically fall back on pre-programmed human interpretive habits, especially when faced with abstract, meta-structural or pre-axiomatic questions.

In practice, AI functions here as a bias-reducing medium.

Limitations and control mechanism

At the same time, AI use introduces another known problem:
AI tends to fill in gaps, generalize or assume plausibility if the questions are unclearly formulated.

For this reason, question formulation is strictly locked:

  • All questions are restricted to internal, formal analysis

  • External criteria are explicitly prohibited

  • Each question is designed to force binary or localizable answers

It is therefore the precision of the questions, not the intelligence of the AI, that determines the quality of the outcome.

Relationship to human review

This AI-based quality control is not a replacement for human review, but a complement that operates at a level where human cognition often encounters difficulties.

As the originator of the question, with many years of experience in testing these formulations in human dialogue, it is well documented how easily even qualified reviewers:

  • project their own frameworks onto the questions

  • answer what they think the question is about, rather than what is actually asked

  • mix in interpretation, evaluation or external context despite instructions

This quality function aims to eliminate precisely these effects, not through authority, but through strict method.

Openness and verifiability

The entire material – including:

  • question formulations

  • instructions

  • AI-generated answers

is published openly and unchanged, so that each external reviewer can:

  • assess the quality of the questions

  • verify that the answers follow from the questions

  • identify any flaws in the construction

The quality control makes no claims to truth or finality.
It makes only one claim:

If the questions are correctly formulated, the answers necessarily follow.

Summary position

This AI-based stress testing is therefore not an argument for the content, but a test of the function of the questions.

The result is not proof.
It is a methodological trail.
